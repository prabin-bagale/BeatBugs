01 — Problem & Vision

The gaps today

Producers earn inconsistently. Artists rely on personal networks. No trusted licensing system exists in Nepal. Payments are fragmented across informal channels. Piracy goes unchecked.

What we're building

A platform where producers upload and sell beats, artists buy legally with instant downloads, and everything — licensing, payments, delivery — is automated and trusted.

07 — Nepal-specific differentiators

Local payment stack

eSewa, Khalti, and IME Pay integrations as first-class citizens — not an afterthought. Stripe for international buyers. Instant payout to producer wallets.

Genre-native discovery

Filters built for NepHop: Lok + hip-hop fusion, Dohori modern, Folk fusion, Drill. "Artist type beat" filter (e.g. Yabesh Thapa type).

Pay-later safety net

For users hesitant about online payments: reserve a beat and complete payment manually. Reduces drop-off from digital payment anxiety.

English ↔ Nepali toggle

Full bilingual UI from day one. Platform language follows user preference, not just the browser locale.

Transaction commission — taken automatically at checkout on every sale. The primary early-stage revenue driver.
Monthly

Ads
Featured beat placements — producers pay for homepage or search result visibility boosts. Phase 2 revenue stream.
10 — Risks & mitigations
High
Low trust in online payments — Offer pay-later option, show verified transaction counts publicly, partner with known Nepali artists for social proof.
High
Piracy / beat sharing — DRM watermark all previews, license verification on download, copyright reporting tool for producers.
Med
Chicken-and-egg supply problem — Manually onboard 20–50 producers pre-launch, offer 0% commission for first 6 months, build catalog before opening to buyers.
Med
Small domestic market — Nepal-first, but India expansion planned for Phase 3. Global reach requires English-first UX and Stripe payment support.
Low
Scope creep — Strict MVP boundary enforced. Features deferred to Phase 2+ with clear re-evaluation triggers (500+ monthly transactions).
11 — Launch roadmap
Phase 1 — Foundation
Phase 2 — Growth
Phase 3 — Expansion

Phase 1 · Months 1–3

Manually onboard 20–50 producers. 0% commission. Build catalog. Soft launch to NepHop community. Fix UX with real user feedback.

Phase 2 · Months 4–8

Partner with established Nepali rappers. TikTok + YouTube Shorts marketing push. Introduce Pro subscriptions. Launch messaging feature.

Phase 3 · Month 9+

Expand to Indian market. Mobile app launch. Vocal marketplace (hire singers/lyricists). Stripe-first for international buyers.

If executed well, this is not a "beat website." It's Nepal's first digital music economy platform — the infrastructure layer that the entire NepHop scene builds on top of.
